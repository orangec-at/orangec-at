# Content Scoring Matrix

Evaluate and prioritize content ideas systematically.

## Scoring Dimensions (1-5 each)

### 1. Relevance (Audience Match)

**5** - Perfect match for primary audience (frontend devs, 2-5 years experience)
**4** - Highly relevant to most of audience
**3** - Somewhat relevant, interests portion of audience
**2** - Tangentially relevant
**1** - Off-topic or marginal interest

### 2. Uniqueness (Differentiation)

**5** - Completely unique perspective, no comparable content exists
**4** - Mostly unique, provides new angle on existing topic
**3** - Some unique value, mostly covered elsewhere
**2** - Slight differentiation from existing content
**1** - Duplicate of widely available content

### 3. SEO Potential (Ranking Opportunity)

**5** - High search volume + low competition + good ranking chance
**4** - Good volume + moderate competition OR moderate volume + low competition
**3** - Moderate volume + moderate competition
**2** - Low volume OR very high competition
**1** - No search volume OR impossible to rank

### 4. Career Value (Portfolio Building)

**5** - Showcases advanced skills, extremely impressive to employers/clients
**4** - Demonstrates strong competence in valuable skills
**3** - Shows solid understanding of common patterns
**2** - Basic skill demonstration
**1** - Minimal portfolio value

## Scoring Examples

### Example 1: "Building Real-Time Chat with Next.js and WebSockets"

- **Relevance**: 5 (frontend devs love real-time features)
- **Uniqueness**: 4 (most guides use Socket.io, yours uses native WebSockets)
- **SEO**: 4 (good volume: "nextjs websocket tutorial", moderate competition)
- **Career**: 5 (impressive portfolio piece, shows advanced skills)
- **Total**: 18/20 - **HIGH PRIORITY**

### Example 2: "What is React?"

- **Relevance**: 3 (beginners, not primary audience)
- **Uniqueness**: 1 (covered exhaustively everywhere)
- **SEO**: 2 (high volume but impossible to rank)
- **Career**: 1 (basic concept, no differentiation)
- **Total**: 7/20 - **SKIP**

### Example 3: "Zustand vs Redux in 2025"

- **Relevance**: 5 (frontend devs choosing state management)
- **Uniqueness**: 4 (timely comparison, 2025 context)
- **SEO**: 4 (good volume, achievable ranking)
- **Career**: 3 (demonstrates knowledge of ecosystem)
- **Total**: 16/20 - **HIGH PRIORITY**

### Example 4: "My Morning Coffee Routine"

- **Relevance**: 1 (completely off-topic)
- **Uniqueness**: 5 (your personal routine is unique)
- **SEO**: 1 (no relevant searches)
- **Career**: 1 (no tech value)
- **Total**: 8/20 - **SKIP**

### Example 5: "Next.js 15 Migration Guide"

- **Relevance**: 5 (all Next.js users need this)
- **Uniqueness**: 3 (official docs exist, but can add personal experience)
- **SEO**: 5 (high volume, achievable: "nextjs 15 migration")
- **Career**: 4 (shows staying current with frameworks)
- **Total**: 17/20 - **HIGH PRIORITY**

## Using the Matrix

### Step 1: Generate Ideas

Brainstorm 10-20 potential topics:
- From trending topics research
- From content gaps analysis
- From audience questions
- From your project experience

### Step 2: Score Each Idea

Go through each dimension systematically:
1. Relevance to audience
2. Uniqueness of angle
3. SEO opportunity
4. Career/portfolio value

### Step 3: Sort by Priority

- 16-20: Add to this month's calendar
- 11-15: Add to next 2-3 months
- 6-10: Maybe list
- 0-5: Discard

### Step 4: Validate Top Picks

For High Priority topics (16-20):
- Use WebSearch to validate SEO potential
- Check seo-optimizer for keyword confirmation
- Ensure you can deliver unique value

### Step 5: Create Content

Focus on high-priority topics first, work down the list

---

## Monthly Review

At month end:
- Score new ideas generated
- Re-score existing ideas (SEO/trends may change)
- Remove outdated low-priority items
- Identify patterns in what scores high

---

## Quick Decision Tool

**When in doubt, ask**:
1. Would my primary audience (frontend dev, 2-5 years) care? → Relevance
2. Can I provide insights others haven't? → Uniqueness
3. Will people search for this? → SEO
4. Does this make my portfolio stronger? → Career

If 3+ answers are "yes" → High priority
If 2 answers are "yes" → Medium priority
If 0-1 answers are "yes" → Skip
