# Job Description Analysis Guide

Extract requirements and optimize your resume for ATS and hiring managers.

---

## JD Anatomy

### 1. Required Skills (CRITICAL)
**Look for**: "Required", "Must have", "Essential"

**Extract**:
- Programming languages
- Frameworks/libraries
- Years of experience
- Domain knowledge

**Example from JD**:
> "Required: 3+ years React, TypeScript, Next.js experience"

**Your Action**: Ensure these keywords appear in your resume

---

### 2. Preferred Skills (NICE-TO-HAVE)
**Look for**: "Preferred", "Nice to have", "Bonus"

**Extract**:
- Additional technologies
- Certifications
- Industry experience

**Strategy**: If you have 60%+ of these, highlight them

---

### 3. Responsibilities (WHAT YOU'LL DO)
**Extract**:
- Day-to-day tasks
- Team collaboration type
- Project ownership level

**Use for**: Tailoring achievement bullets to match work style

---

### 4. Company Keywords
**Extract**:
- Company values ("fast-paced", "collaborative", "innovative")
- Product keywords (e.g., "SaaS", "B2B", "e-commerce")
- Team size signals ("small team", "startup", "enterprise")

**Use for**: Cover letter personalization

---

## Skill Matching System

### Scoring Your Fit

\`\`\`
Must-Have Skills Matched / Total Must-Have = Match Score
\`\`\`

**90-100% Match**: Apply immediately
- You have all required skills
- Action: Emphasize depth of experience

**75-89% Match**: Strong candidate
- Missing 1-2 requirements
- Action: Highlight transferable skills, show learning ability

**60-74% Match**: Stretch role
- Missing several requirements
- Action: Focus on related experience, emphasize fast learning

**Below 60%**: Build skills first
- Significant gaps
- Action: Consider junior roles or skill development

## ATS Keyword Optimization

### How ATS Works

**Scans for**:
- Exact keyword matches (case-insensitive)
- Tech stack names
- Years of experience
- Education/certifications

**Rejects if**:
- Missing critical keywords
- Weird formatting (tables, images)
- Unreadable fonts

## Company Research Checklist

**Before applying**, research:

- [ ] **Product**: What do they build? Who uses it?
- [ ] **Tech Stack**: Official blog/careers page often lists it
- [ ] **Company Size**: Startup vs scale-up vs enterprise
- [ ] **Recent News**: Funding, product launches, press
- [ ] **Culture**: LinkedIn posts, Glassdoor, blog

**Use in**:
- Cover letter: "I'm excited about [recent launch]..."
- Interview prep: Intelligent questions

---

## Red Flags in JDs

**Avoid if you see**:
- "Rockstar", "Ninja", "Guru" (unprofessional)
- Unrealistic requirements ("5+ years React 19" when React 19 is 6 months old)
- Excessive responsibilities with junior salary
- "Must work 60+ hours" (poor work-life balance)
- Vague job description (company doesn't know what they want)
