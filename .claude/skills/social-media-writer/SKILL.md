## Core Principles

### 1. Platform-Specific Optimization

Each platform has unique format requirements, character limits, and audience expectations:

- **Twitter/X**: Threads (7-10 tweets), hooks, numbered lists, code snippets as images
- **Instagram**: Carousels (8-10 slides), visual-first, story-based captions, hashtags
- **LinkedIn**: Professional articles (1000-1500 words), industry insights, networking CTAs
- **Facebook**: Community posts (200-400 words), questions, group sharing

### 2. Hook-First Writing

Capture attention in the first 3 seconds:
- Lead with the problem or surprising insight
- Use numbers ("5 ways...", "3x faster...")
- Ask provocative questions
- Share contrarian opinions
- Reference pain points

### 3. Value-Dense Content

Every word must earn its place:
- Cut fluff and filler words
- Use concrete examples over abstract concepts
- Include actionable takeaways
- Show, don't just tell
- End with clear next steps

### 4. CTA Strategy

Drive traffic back to your blog/portfolio:
- Natural CTAs that provide value ("Full guide: [link]")
- Multiple touchpoints (thread end, bio, comments)
- Platform-specific CTA formats
- Track click-through rates

### LinkedIn Article Workflow

**Goal**: Transform blog post into professional article (1000-1500 words)

#### Step 1: Adapt Tone

**Blog Tone â†’ LinkedIn Tone**:
- Casual â†’ Professional (but still personal)
- "I built this hack" â†’ "I developed this solution"
- Code-first â†’ Business impact-first
- Tutorial â†’ Case study with lessons learned

#### Step 2: Structure for LinkedIn

**LinkedIn Article Structure**:

```markdown
**Title**: [Clear benefit] - [Context]
Example: "How I Reduced CSS Bundle Size by 50% - Lessons from 3 Production Apps"

**Hook** (2-3 sentences):
State the problem and your credentials

**Context** (2 paragraphs):
- Industry challenge
- Why this matters for business/teams

**The Challenge** (2-3 paragraphs):
- Specific problem you faced
- What you tried that didn't work
- Stakes and constraints

**The Solution** (3-5 sections):
Section 1: Approach #1
- What you did
- Why it worked
- Results

Section 2: Approach #2
[Repeat pattern]

**Results** (1 paragraph with metrics):
- Performance improvements
- Business impact
- Team benefits

**Key Takeaways** (Numbered list):
1. Lesson #1 with actionable insight
2. Lesson #2 with industry context
3. Lesson #3 with recommendation

**Call to Action**:
Invitation to connect, comment, or visit blog for technical details
```

#### Step 3: Add Professional Polish

**LinkedIn Best Practices**:
- First-person narrative ("I", "we", "our team")
- Industry context and broader implications
- Lessons learned and failures (authenticity)
- Professional but conversational tone
- Data and metrics prominently featured
- Clear value proposition in first 2 sentences

#### Step 4: Visual Elements

- **Hero image**: Professional screenshot or diagram
- **Inline images**: 2-3 supporting visuals (metrics, architecture, before/after)
- **Code blocks**: Minimal, only key snippets
- **Charts/graphs**: If showing performance improvements
